ADR-008

DMIP AS PRIMARY PRODUCT

Status : Approved

Date : 2026-06-24

Version : 1.0.0

---

Context

AIOPS-OS is being developed as the foundational operating system for the DOKB technology ecosystem.

The ecosystem may eventually support multiple platforms and services.

However, the organization requires a clear first destination for architecture, governance, and engineering efforts.

Without a primary product focus, development risks becoming fragmented.

---

Problem Statement

Multiple opportunities exist, including:

- organizational systems;
- analytics platforms;
- intelligence systems;
- mobility services;
- ride hailing platforms.

Attempting to pursue all initiatives simultaneously would dilute resources and slow progress.

A primary product focus was required.

---

Decision

DMIP (DOKB Mobility Intelligence Platform) is designated as the first primary product of the AIOPS-OS ecosystem.

All foundational architecture work should align with future DMIP requirements.

DMIP becomes the primary target platform for:

- governance preparation;
- architecture planning;
- engineering standards;
- AI orchestration capabilities.

---

Definition

DMIP is an intelligence-driven mobility platform designed to support:

- transportation analysis;
- mobility intelligence;
- driver economics;
- operational analytics;
- policy evaluation;
- decision support.

DMIP serves as the practical implementation of AIOPS-OS principles.

---

Strategic Objectives

DMIP aims to provide:

- evidence-based transportation analysis;
- data-driven advocacy support;
- operational intelligence;
- mobility insights;
- policy support capabilities.

The platform supports long-term transportation ecosystem improvement.

---

Why DMIP First

DMIP aligns directly with:

- DOKB mission;
- driver advocacy needs;
- transportation policy challenges;
- mobility intelligence requirements.

The platform provides immediate strategic value while validating AIOPS-OS architecture.

---

Alternatives Considered

Alternative A

Build Multiple Products Simultaneously

Advantages:

- broader experimentation.

Disadvantages:

- diluted focus;
- increased complexity;
- slower execution.

Decision:

Rejected.

---

Alternative B

Ride Hailing Platform First

Advantages:

- direct commercial potential.

Disadvantages:

- high operational complexity;
- insufficient intelligence foundation.

Decision:

Rejected.

---

Alternative C

DMIP First

Advantages:

- aligned with mission;
- validates architecture;
- supports advocacy;
- creates reusable intelligence capabilities.

Decision:

Accepted.

---

Consequences

Positive Consequences:

- clear development focus;
- improved prioritization;
- stronger architectural alignment;
- measurable platform objectives.

Negative Consequences:

- other initiatives receive lower priority;
- some opportunities may be delayed.

The benefits outweigh the drawbacks.

---

Strategic Impact

DMIP becomes the central destination for:

- AI orchestration;
- intelligence systems;
- mobility analytics;
- transportation research;
- policy evaluation.

The platform validates the AIOPS-OS architecture.

---

Future Expansion

Successful DMIP implementation may enable:

- regional intelligence systems;
- transportation observatories;
- operational analytics platforms;
- future ride hailing services.

DMIP acts as the bridge between governance and future mobility products.
