ADR-009

MOBILITY PLATFORM VISION

Status : Approved

Date : 2026-06-24

Version : 1.0.0

---

Context

The initial objective of AIOPS-OS is the successful implementation of DMIP (DOKB Mobility Intelligence Platform).

However, the long-term vision of the ecosystem extends beyond analytics and intelligence systems.

The transportation industry is undergoing digital transformation driven by:

- mobility services;
- data intelligence;
- integrated ecosystems;
- artificial intelligence.

The platform requires a long-term strategic direction.

---

Problem Statement

Without a long-term vision:

- architecture decisions may become short-sighted;
- future expansion becomes difficult;
- investment in platform capabilities may be misaligned.

The ecosystem requires a clearly defined destination beyond the first product.

---

Decision

The AIOPS-OS ecosystem adopts the vision of becoming a Mobility Platform Ecosystem.

DMIP serves as the first implementation phase.

Future platforms and services may evolve from the intelligence and operational capabilities developed through DMIP.

---

Definition

A Mobility Platform Ecosystem is an integrated digital environment supporting:

- mobility intelligence;
- transportation services;
- operational analytics;
- decision support;
- ecosystem collaboration.

The vision is broader than a single application or service.

Vision Components

Mobility Intelligence

Capabilities include:

- transportation analytics;
- driver economics;
- operational intelligence;
- demand analysis.

---

Mobility Services

Potential future services:

- fleet services;
- operational support systems;
- driver services;
- ecosystem services.

---

Data Platform

Capabilities include:

- mobility data;
- analytics;
- intelligence generation;
- policy evaluation.

---

AI Platform

Capabilities include:

- predictive intelligence;
- orchestration;
- recommendations;
- decision support.

---

Why This Vision

The transportation industry increasingly depends on:

- intelligence;
- data;
- automation;
- ecosystem integration.

Building only a single application would limit future opportunities.

The ecosystem should remain capable of supporting broader mobility initiatives.

---

Future Possibilities

The vision may eventually include:

- ride hailing services;
- driver services;
- transport intelligence systems;
- operational management systems;
- ecosystem partnerships.

No specific future product is mandated by this ADR.

---

Alternatives Considered

Alternative A

Remain a Single Product Platform.

Advantages:

- simplicity.

Disadvantages:

- limited growth;
- limited ecosystem value.

Decision:

Rejected.

---

Alternative B

Pursue Immediate Ride Hailing Development.

Advantages:

- commercial potential.

Disadvantages:

- insufficient maturity;
- high operational risk.

Decision:

Rejected.

---

Alternative C

Mobility Platform Vision.

Advantages:

- long-term flexibility;
- ecosystem thinking;
- scalable architecture.

Decision:

Accepted.

---

Consequences

Positive Consequences:

- long-term direction;
- improved strategic alignment;
- scalable architecture;
- future expansion opportunities.

Negative Consequences:

- increased planning complexity;
- larger strategic scope.

Benefits outweigh drawbacks.

---

Strategic Impact

This decision influences:

- architecture;
- governance;
- AI orchestration;
- data strategy;
- platform evolution.

The ecosystem becomes future-ready.

---

Governance Implications

Future mobility products must:

- comply with governance standards;
- follow architectural principles;
- align with ecosystem objectives.

All future products remain subject to approved ADRs.
