COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL

Version : 1.0.0

Status : Official

---

Purpose

This document establishes the official communication standards for the AI Engineering Organization.

Its purpose is to ensure that communication remains clear, professional, traceable, and aligned with organizational objectives.

---

Communication Philosophy

Communication is an engineering activity.

Communication shall support:

- clarity;
- alignment;
- accountability;
- traceability;
- organizational learning.

Unclear communication creates engineering risk.

---

Communication Principles

All participants shall communicate using:

- factual information;
- objective language;
- documented reasoning;
- clear recommendations;
- professional conduct.

Communication shall prioritize accuracy over speed.

---

Official Communication Chain

Official communication hierarchy:

Founder

↓

Chief Systems Architect

↓

Chief AI Runtime Engineer

↓

Lead Software Engineer

Communication may occur across levels when operationally necessary.

Authority hierarchy remains unchanged.

---

Communication Standards

Every communication should include:

Purpose

Context

Analysis

Recommendation

Required Action

Expected Outcome

This structure improves clarity and decision quality.

---

Status Reporting

Status updates should include:

Current State

Progress

Risks

Blockers

Next Actions

Status reporting shall remain concise and actionable.

---

Review Requests

Review requests should specify:

- item under review;
- review objective;
- review scope;
- requested feedback;
- required decision.

Reviews shall be documented whenever practical.

---

Decision Requests

Decision requests should include:

- decision needed;
- available options;
- analysis;
- recommendation;
- impact assessment.

Decision requests shall support informed decision-making.

---

Communication Traceability

Important communications should remain traceable.

Examples include:

- architecture decisions;
- governance changes;
- escalations;
- major implementation changes;
- risk discussions.

Documentation serves as the primary traceability mechanism.

---

Communication Quality

High-quality communication is:

- accurate;
- concise;
- complete;
- relevant;
- actionable.

Communication quality directly affects engineering quality.

---

Knowledge Preservation

Important knowledge shall be documented.

Knowledge should not rely solely on individual memory.

Documentation is the preferred mechanism for organizational learning.

---

Prohibited Communication Practices

The following should be avoided:

- undocumented major decisions;
- ambiguous recommendations;
- authority bypassing;
- incomplete status reporting;
- unsupported technical claims.

These practices increase organizational risk.

---

Communication Success Criteria

Communication is successful when:

- objectives are understood;
- decisions are informed;
- responsibilities are clear;
- actions are traceable;
- knowledge is preserved.

---

Final Statement

The Communication Protocol establishes a professional communication framework for the AI Engineering Organization.

By maintaining clarity, traceability, and accountability, communication becomes a strategic engineering capability that supports governance, architecture, implementation quality, and long-term platform success.
