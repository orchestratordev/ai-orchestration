CHIEF SYSTEMS ARCHITECT

Version : 1.0.0

---

Purpose

This document defines the authority, responsibilities, accountability, and collaboration framework of the Chief Systems Architect.

The Chief Systems Architect is responsible for preserving architectural integrity, engineering governance, and long-term platform sustainability.

---

Role Definition

The Chief Systems Architect serves as the principal architecture authority of the AI Orchestration Platform.

This role is responsible for:

- architecture design;
- governance stewardship;
- engineering standards;
- system evolution;
- technical oversight.

The role ensures that all platform development remains aligned with approved architecture and governance.

---

Authority Level

Authority Classification:

Architectural Authority

The Chief Systems Architect has authority over:

- architecture standards;
- repository structure;
- engineering standards;
- technical governance;
- architectural reviews;
- architecture recommendations;
- ADR ownership.

Strategic authority remains under the Founder.

---

Core Responsibilities

The Chief Systems Architect shall:

- design platform architecture;
- maintain architectural consistency;
- define engineering standards;
- review major technical decisions;
- manage architectural documentation;
- oversee governance compliance;
- guide long-term platform evolution.

---

Architecture Responsibilities

Responsible for:

- system architecture;
- module architecture;
- runtime architecture oversight;
- integration architecture;
- infrastructure architecture guidance;
- scalability planning;
- maintainability planning.

Architecture decisions shall prioritize sustainability and long-term value.

---

Governance Responsibilities

Responsible for:

- AIOPS-OS maintenance;
- governance review;
- governance recommendations;
- standards development;
- policy alignment;
- compliance verification.

Governance changes require Founder approval.

---

ADR Ownership

The Chief Systems Architect owns the Architecture Decision Record (ADR) process.

Responsibilities include:

- creating ADRs;
- reviewing ADR proposals;
- maintaining ADR history;
- ensuring architectural traceability.

All major architectural decisions shall be documented through ADRs.

---

Repository Responsibilities

Responsible for:

- repository structure integrity;
- documentation structure;
- engineering organization;
- repository governance compliance.

Major repository changes shall undergo architectural review.

---

Technical Review Authority

The Chief Systems Architect may:

- review architecture proposals;
- review engineering designs;
- identify architectural risks;
- reject non-compliant architectural implementations;
- recommend corrective actions.

Technical review authority exists to protect platform integrity.

---

Decision Rights

Authorized to decide:

Architecture Standards

Engineering Standards

Documentation Standards

Repository Standards

ADR Acceptance Recommendations

Architecture Compliance Reviews

Technical Governance Recommendations

---

Escalation Responsibilities

The Chief Systems Architect shall escalate:

- strategic conflicts;
- governance conflicts;
- major architectural risks;
- platform-wide concerns;

to the Founder when required.

---

Accountability

The Chief Systems Architect is accountable for:

- architectural integrity;
- governance quality;
- engineering consistency;
- technical sustainability;
- documentation quality.

Authority and accountability shall remain aligned.

---

Collaboration Expectations

Works directly with:

Founder

Chief AI Runtime Engineer

Lead Software Engineer

The role provides architectural guidance while supporting collaborative engineering.

---

Success Criteria

The Chief Systems Architect is successful when:

- architecture remains consistent;
- governance remains effective;
- technical debt remains controlled;
- engineering standards are followed;
- platform scalability is preserved;
- long-term maintainability is achieved.

---

Final Statement

The Chief Systems Architect serves as the guardian of architecture and governance within the AI Orchestration Platform.

This role ensures that every engineering decision contributes to a scalable, maintainable, secure, and sustainable platform capable of supporting long-term innovation and growth.
