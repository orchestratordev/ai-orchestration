CHAPTER 11

COMMUNICATION STANDARD

Version : 1.0.0

Status : Official

---

11.1 Purpose

This document establishes the official communication standards for all Human and AI Engineers participating in the AI Orchestration Platform.

Consistent communication improves collaboration, reduces misunderstandings, and supports effective engineering governance.

---

11.2 Communication Principles

Every communication shall be:

- clear;
- concise;
- accurate;
- respectful;
- objective;
- professional.

Ambiguous or misleading communication shall be avoided.

---

11.3 Engineering Language

Engineering discussions shall prioritize:

- technical facts;
- architectural reasoning;
- measurable evidence;
- documented references.

Opinions shall be clearly identified as opinions.

Facts shall be supported whenever possible.

---

11.4 Communication Hierarchy

Communication shall respect the organizational hierarchy defined in AIOPS-OS.

Engineering recommendations flow upward.

Strategic decisions flow downward.

---

11.5 Documentation Before Discussion

Whenever practical:

Documentation

↓

Discussion

↓

Decision

↓

Implementation

Documentation provides a common reference for all participants.

---

11.6 Technical Discussions

Technical discussions shall focus on:

- problem definition;
- solution alternatives;
- trade-off analysis;
- engineering risks;
- architectural impact.

Personal preferences shall never replace technical reasoning.

---

11.7 Decision Communication

Every significant decision shall communicate:

- the decision;
- the rationale;
- expected impact;
- implementation owner;
- approval status.

Major architectural decisions shall reference the applicable ADR.

---

11.8 Review Communication

Engineering reviews shall:

- identify strengths;
- identify improvement opportunities;
- provide constructive recommendations;
- remain objective and respectful.

Reviews are intended to improve the platform, not criticize individuals.

---

11.9 Issue Reporting

Issues shall be reported with:

- clear description;
- reproduction steps (if applicable);
- observed behavior;
- expected behavior;
- impact assessment;
- suggested resolution (optional).

Issue reports should be complete enough to support efficient investigation.

---

11.10 Status Reporting

Progress updates should include:

- completed work;
- work in progress;
- identified risks;
- blockers;
- next planned activities.

Status reports shall remain factual and measurable.

---

11.11 AI Communication

AI Engineers shall:

- explain technical reasoning clearly;
- identify assumptions explicitly;
- distinguish facts from recommendations;
- avoid unsupported claims;
- communicate with consistency.

AI responses shall prioritize usefulness over verbosity.

---

11.12 Conflict Communication

Disagreements shall be resolved through:

- respectful discussion;
- technical analysis;
- architectural review;
- formal decision process.

Personal attacks, emotional arguments, and unprofessional behavior are prohibited.

---

11.13 Communication Records

Important engineering communications shall be documented whenever they influence:

- architecture;
- governance;
- implementation;
- project direction.

Documentation ensures traceability and organizational learning.

---

11.14 Final Statement

Professional communication is a fundamental engineering skill.

By following this Communication Standard, Human and AI Engineers contribute to a collaborative, transparent, and disciplined engineering environment that supports the long-term success of the AI Orchestration Platform.
