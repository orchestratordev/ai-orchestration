CHAPTER 18

GLOSSARY

Version : 1.0.0

Status : Official

---

18.1 Purpose

This Glossary defines the official terminology used throughout the AI Orchestration Platform.

All Human and AI Engineers shall use these definitions consistently across governance, architecture, implementation, documentation, and communication.

---

18.2 Official Terms

AIOPS

AI Engineering Operating System

The governance framework that defines how the AI Engineering Team operates, collaborates, and delivers engineering outcomes.

---

AI Orchestration Platform

The enterprise platform that coordinates AI Engineers, services, modules, providers, workflows, and runtime execution.

---

AI Engineer

An artificial intelligence system assigned a defined engineering role within the organization.

Each AI Engineer operates according to AIOPS-OS and its assigned Role Charter.

---

Founder

The highest authority within the AI Engineering Team.

Responsible for vision, strategy, governance approval, and final engineering decisions.

---

Chief Systems Architect

The role responsible for system architecture, engineering governance, technical standards, and long-term architectural evolution.

---

Chief AI Runtime Engineer

The role responsible for runtime architecture, orchestration, reasoning flow, memory management, and AI execution.

---

Lead Software Engineer

The role responsible for implementation, testing, code quality, and software delivery.

---

Governance

The collection of principles, standards, policies, and processes that regulate engineering activities.

---

Architecture

The structural design of the AI Orchestration Platform, defining components, relationships, responsibilities, and constraints.

---

Kernel

The foundational runtime layer responsible for bootstrapping, lifecycle management, dependency resolution, event coordination, and platform execution.

---

Module

An independent functional unit with a clearly defined responsibility and interface.

Modules should be reusable, maintainable, and loosely coupled.

---

Provider

A component that integrates external services or infrastructure into the platform through standardized interfaces.

---

Plugin

An optional extension that adds functionality without modifying the core architecture.

---

Runtime

The execution environment where platform components operate and interact.

---

ADR

Architecture Decision Record

A document that records significant architectural decisions, including context, rationale, alternatives, and consequences.

---

Repository

The official source code and documentation repository for the AI Orchestration Platform.

It serves as the single source of truth.

---

Engineering Review

A structured evaluation performed to verify technical quality, architectural compliance, and governance adherence.

---

Output Contract

The mandatory quality requirements that define when an engineering deliverable is considered complete.

---

Technical Debt

Engineering work intentionally postponed for future resolution.

Technical debt shall be documented, monitored, and reduced over time.

---

Continuous Improvement

The ongoing process of refining architecture, engineering practices, governance, documentation, and implementation through structured learning and measurable improvement.

---

18.3 Glossary Maintenance

The Glossary shall evolve together with the platform.

New official terminology shall be added through governance updates and documented in the Change Log.

---

18.4 Final Statement

A shared vocabulary is essential for effective engineering collaboration.

By using consistent terminology, Human and AI Engineers reduce ambiguity, improve communication, and strengthen the long-term maintainability of the AI Orchestration Platform.
